5.2 Disaster Risk Assessment Table (DRA Table)

5.2 Disaster Risk Assessment Table (DRA Table)


At this point, the EDTs for all exposure units and hazards must be filled in. Using the excel template from HLURB-PDG, scores for each component of risk (i.e. exposure, sensitivity, etc.) and eventually the final risk score are automatically computed. LGUs and other users should take note that the templates provided are not prescriptive, and they can be revised with proper methodological justifications to ensure that the risk assessment process suits the proper context of each LGU.


Computation of risk


Instructions:


    • Fill in  EDTs for all exposure units and for all hazards (refer to Step 3).
    • The final risk score (i.e. low, medium, high; last column) is computed based on the values for the following components:
      • Hazard
      • Exposure
      • Sensitivities
      • Adaptive capacities
    • The scores are then automatically computed for the following:
      • Hazard Exposure
      • Vulnerability
      • Severity of Consequence
      • Risk (including risk category)

Verification and adjustment

      • Once all the values and scores are provided in the table, LGU and other users are encouraged to verify and adjust the results (if necessary) particularly the risk scores (and risk category) using their local knowledge. The last columns of the table should then be verified and adjusted. In some cases, the results may not be consistent to what local people know or experience. There are several explanations such as:
        • Values in the preceding columns are incorrect. Entries should then be verified and corrected.
        • There are other factors that are not considered in the assessment. For example, a barangay came out "highly exposed to flood" because of exposure to flood according to the the hazard map. But the assessment did not consider that there is a new flood mitigating measure that significantly reduces the exposure to flood.
        • In case of adjustment of score, add 2 columns to the table:
          • A column to reflect adjustment on the score or risk category (e.g. from low to high, or high to medium, etc.).
          • A column to explain the adjustment made (flood mitigating measure is in place reducing exposure to flood).
