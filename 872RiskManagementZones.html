8.7.2 Risk Management Zones


Risk management zones are areas exposed to hazards or at risk and development and human activities are restricted. These areas are subject to Areas for Priority Action (APAs) which can be described as existing urban use areas within hazard prone areas (regardless of risk level) based on the results of the CDRA risk mapping. The determination of APAs or risk management options depend on the different risk scenarios (Table 8.7.2):


Table 8.7.2: APAs or risk management options for each scenario (HLURB, 2015; p. 262-264)


Scenario

APAs or risk management options (examples)

Risk areas where current and future capacities of property owners are not commensurate to the costs

Requiring property owners to address risks within a given time frame (LGUs can adopt a 10-year period with consultation with owners).

Land acquisition or swapping where LGU offers other suitable areas in the locality. LGU assumes responsibility of the property for allocation to other suitable uses.

Relocation of existing households through resettlement.

Instituting local ordinances to increase property taxes and revenues derived from property owners, outside the risk management districts where revenues can be redirected in area redevelopment.

Risk areas where current and future capacities of property owners are commensurate to the costs

Property owner is required to submit a structural engineering assessment to be conducted by a licensed structural engineer.

Require property owners to address risks within a given time frame (LGUs can adopt a 10 year period with consultation with owners).

Tax holiday for a period of five years with the condition that property owners employ the required structural mitigation measures/retrofitting.

When property is significantly damaged within the 10-year period, property owner will be allowed to repair and rehabilitate the structure provided that it follows the minimum standards on hazard resistant design.

Risk areas where the only recourse is to relocate

Implement easements as an effective strategy in managing risks

Field Demarcation/Delineation of hazard prone areas

Protection of forests/watersheds

Protection and rehabilitation of ecologically sensitive and critical habitats

Synergy and convergence of protection policies across Municipalities/ Cities
