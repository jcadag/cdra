1.2 Make an inventory of hazard maps


The inventory of hazard maps provides an overview of available dataset on hazard (e.g. GIS data on hazards) that is useful for hazard mapping and risk assessment.  


Instructions:


    • Collect the necessary data and information on hazards from government agencies and reliable open-access data (i.e. http://www.geoportal.gov.ph/,http://gdis.mgb.gov.ph/mgbpublic, http://noah.dost.gov.ph/).
    • Create a database for the hazard maps using a Geographic Information System (GIS) software (i.e. QGIS, ArcGIS). Collect hazard maps in GIS format (vector or raster data).
    • Standardize map display by using the same base map for all hazards with the same scale and relatively the same arrangement of legend (Figure 2 and Figure 3)
    • Available seismic and volcanic hazard maps from PHIVOLCS (i.e. ground shaking, ground rupture, earthquake-induced landslide, liquefaction, storm surge and tsunami) should also be used to assess potential impacts to elements at risk.
    • PHIVOLCS also offers hazard assessment tool such as REDAS (Rapid Earthquake Damage Assessment System) that can be used to produce probabilistic seismic hazard maps. This is particularly important in planning for worst case scenario in case of occurrence of major hazards (i.e. 7.2 magnitude earthquake in Sagbayan, Bohol in 2013). PHIVOLCS regularly conduct trainings on REDAS to LGUs and other DRR stakeholders. For inquiries, visit PHIVOLCS website at www.phivolcs.dost.gov.ph.
        • Recently, DOST-PHIVOLCS has launched Hazard Hunter - an online web platform that displays information on hazard and critical infrastructure such as schools. Refer to 2.1.C Geological hazards for information about hazard maps and information of PHIVOLCS.
