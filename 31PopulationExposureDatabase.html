3.1 Population Exposure Database

3.1 Population Exposure Database


Establish population exposure database :


  • Population EDT (Exposure Database Table) - produce and complete table that consist proper indicators to assess exposure to hazards and risk for population.
  • Population EDM (Exposure Database Maps) - produce maps that show location of residential areas and hazards (preferably probabilistic hazard maps).


Population EDT


The Population EDT compiles the indicators for vulnerability and risk computation for all exposure units in all barangays exposed to particular hazards. It is a long table that requires inputs such as statistics, GIS-derived and formula-based values. Watch this video  for an orientation on the EDT.


For Population EDT, the LGU or user may use any of the templates:


    • Population EDT (with CBMS or Household-Level data)
    • Population EDT (with no CBMS or Household-Level data)


Instructions:


    • EDT has four major criteria that are used to compute vulnerability and risk:
      • Hazard
      • Exposure
      • Sensitivity
      • Adaptive Capacity


    • Identify indicators for each criterion (refer to sample Population EDT above)
      • HLURB has already proposed indicators for each criterion. LGUs are encouraged to change or replace these indicators depending on their context.
      • Refer to 3.1.1 Menu of indicators for population exposure units. Other indicators may be included or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.


    • Fill in the Population EDT
      • Barangay (column A)
        • List only those barangays that are exposed to the particular hazard being assessed (e.g. flood in this example)


      • Hazard (columns B to E)
        • Fill in columns B to E. Watch this video  for a demonstration.
        • Refer to Hazard Scoring guide.


      • Exposure (columns F to O)
        • Fill in columns F to H based on actual data on the barangay (e.g. number of population for each barangay based on recent census).
        • Fill in columns L to N if using actual household data (i.e. GPS point of houses with population data) to compute number of exposed population (Population EDT with CBMS or Household-Level data). Watch this video  for a demonstration.
        • Fill in columns I to N if estimating number of exposed population using building footprints (Population EDT with no CBMS or Household-Level data). Watch this  for a demonstration.
        • Exposure score is auto computed based on the formula. Refer to Exposure Score guide. The formula can be adjusted with proper justification.


      • Sensitivity (columns P to AH)
        • Fill in columns P to AG based on the selected indicators.
        • Refer to 3.1.1 Menu of indicators for population exposure units for the indicators on sensitivity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
        • The values (number or percentage) for each indicator should be based on the number of exposed population to a particular hazard. For example, if there are 300 people exposed to a high level flood out of 1000 people in the barangay (total population), then the value for each indicator should be based on the 300 people, not for the total 1000 people in the barangay.
        • OPTION: If there is no data at the household level, the average of the barangay may be used. But this should be done with the assumption that the distribution of people with similar characteristics is homogenous i.e. the percentage of informal settlers or young dependent living in one part of the barangay is similar to all parts of the barangay.
        • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
        • The average sensitivity score shall be computed at the last column of sensitivity section (i.e. column AH).
        • Refer to Sensitivity Scoring guide.


      • Adaptive capacity (columns AI to AS)
        • Fill in columns AI to AS based on the indicators of adaptive capacity.
        • Refer to 3.1.1 Menu of indicators for population exposure units for the indicators on adaptive capacity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
        • Similar to indicators of sensitivities above, the values (number or percentage) should preferably be based on the number of exposed population to a particular hazard. For example, if there are 300 people exposed to high level of flood out of 1000 people in the barangay (total population), then the value for each indicator should be based on the 300 people, not for the total 1000 people in the barangay.
        • OPTION: If there is no data at the household level, the average of the barangay may be used. But this should be done with the assumption that the distribution of people with similar characteristics is homogenous i.e. the percentage of informal settlers or young dependent living in one part of the barangay is similar to all parts of the barangay.
        • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
        • The average adaptive capacity score shall be computed at the last column of adaptive capacity section (i.e. column AS)
        • Refer to Adaptive Capacity Scoring guide.


    • Once EDT for population is filled in, the succeeding columns for vulnerability (columns (AU to AV) and risk (AW-AY) shall be automatically computed. The computation of vulnerability and risk scores are explained in Step 4 and Step 5.


    • Repeat the process to complete all Population EDT for all hazards (i.e. flood, landslide, storm surge, groundshaking, etc.).

Population EDM


The Population EDM shows the location and distribution of exposure units (i.e. population) and hazard prone areas (i.e. flood prone areas).


Instructions:


1. Identify and map residential areas.

    • Collect spatial data on the residential areas of the city/ municipality.
    • Residential areas may be represented by either building footprints or point locations of the houses with attribute data (number of residents on each house and their socioeconomic characteristics).
    • Produce a map that shows the location of the residential areas. Aside from municipal-scaled map, LGUs are encouraged to produce barangay- or cluster-scaled map for better risk analysis.


2. Identify and map flood prone areas

    • Collect spatial data on the flood prone areas.
    • LGUs and other users are encouraged to use probabilistic hazard maps (refer to probabilistic hazard maps). Use spatial data on hazard maps collected in Step 1.2.


3. Assess exposure of population to flood hazard

    • Using GIS, overlay flood hazard map on the population data.
    • Utilize GIS geoprocessing tools such as "intersect/clipping" "select" and "statistics," among other geoprocessing tools, to identify houses or households that are exposed to hazards.
      • This video  demonstrates the use of "select" and "statistics" to compute for number of exposed population to flood using point data.
      • This video  demonstrates the use of "intersect/clipping" and "statistics" to compute for number of exposed population to flood using point data.
    • Produce a map showing exposure of population to flood.
