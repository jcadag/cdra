7.1 CDRA Compilation


This section compiles CDRA results from 6-steps for mainstreaming to CLUP, CDP and other development plans. LGUs are encouraged to produce a compilation of CDRA results (herein referred to as CDRA Compilation).


Instructions:


    • Organize the key results per step. The proposed outline for the compilation follows the key steps and sub-steps of CDRA. Copy and paste key results such as tables and maps from each step. Provide a short explanation or interpretation on the result.


Table 7.1: Suggested outline for CDRA Compilation


    • Step 1 - Collect and organize climate change and hazard information
      • 1.1 Local Climate Change Projection
        • 1.1.A Seasonal temperature and seasonal rainfall changes
        • 1.1.B Number of extreme events
        • 1.1.C General changes expected in climate variables and information
      • 1.2 Make an inventory of hazard maps
        • 1.2.A Produce maps for hydrometeorological hazards
        • 1.2.B Produce maps for geological hazards
      • 1.3 Gather data on historical disaster damage and loss data
      • 1.4 Produce barangay-level hazard susceptibility inventory matrix
    • Step 2 - Scope the potential impacts of hazards and climate change
      • 2.1 Climate impact chain analysis
      • 2.2 Summary of potential impacts of climate stimuli and hazards on exposed units
    • Step 3 - Develop exposure database (include as annex)
      • 3.1 Population Exposure Database
      • 3.2 Urban Uses Exposure Database
      • 3.3 Natural Resource-based Production Areas Exposure Database
      • 3.4 Critical Point Facilities Exposure Database
      • 3.5 Lifeline Utilities Exposure Database
      • 3.6 Other Elements Exposure Database
    • Step 4 - Climate Risk Assessment (CRA)
      • 4.2 Climate Risk Assessment Table (verified and adjusted)
      • 4.3 Climate Risk Assessment Mapping
    • Step 5 - Disaster Risk Assessment (DRA)
      • 5.2 Disaster Risk Assessment Table (verified and adjusted)
      • 5.3  Disaster Risk Assessment Mapping
    • Step 6 - Summarize findings
      • 6.1 Summarize Findings
      • 6.2 Identification and Mapping of Major Decision Areas (MDAs)

Interpretation of results: a huge portion of the city, both urban and rural barangays, are highly exposed to flood. Many urban barangays and communities particularly those situated in low-lying coastal areas and major rivers in or near the CBD are exposed to different degrees of flooding following the classification of MGB. The total land area classified as highly susceptible to flooding is about 12,186.46 hectares comprised of at least 15 coastal barangays.
