3.3 Natural Resource-based Production Areas Exposure Database

3.3 Natural Resource-based Production Areas Exposure Database


Establish Natural Resource-based Production Areas exposure database:


  • Natural Resource-based Production Areas EDT (Exposure Database Table) - produce and complete table that consist proper indicators to assess exposure to hazards and risk for Natural Resource based Production Areas.
  • Natural Resource-based Production Areas EDM (Exposure Database Maps) - produce maps that show location and distribution of Natural Resource based Production Areas and hazards (preferably probabilistic hazard maps).


Natural Resource-based Production Areas EDT


The EDT compiles the indicators for vulnerability and risk computation for all exposure units in all barangays exposed to particular hazards. It is a long table that requires inputs such as statistics, GIS-derived and formula-based values. Watch this video  for an orientation on the EDT.


Instructions:


1. EDT has four major criteria that are used to compute vulnerability and risk:

    • Hazard
    • Exposure
    • Sensitivity
    • Adaptive Capacity


2. Identify indicators for each criterion (refer to sample Natural Resource based Production Areas exposure units database EDT above)

    • HLURB has already proposed indicators for each criterion. LGUs are encouraged to change or replace these indicators depending on their context.
    • Refer to 3.3.1 Menu of indicators for Natural Resource-based Production Areas exposure units. Other indicators may be included or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.


3. Fill in the Natural Resource based Production Areas EDT

    • Barangay and land use classification (column A-B)
      • List only those barangays and land uses that are exposed to the particular hazard being assessed (e.g. flood in this example; and all land use categories exposed to flood e.g. fishpond, mangroves, etc.)
    • Hazard (columns C to F)
      • Fill in columns C to F. Watch this video  for a demonstration.
      • Refer to Hazard Scoring guide.
    • Exposure (columns G to P)
      • Fill in columns G to P based on actual data on land use in the barangay (e.g. total barangay allocation in hectare for fishpond and forest).
      • Exposure score is auto computed based on the formula. Refer to Exposure Score guide. The formula can be adjusted with proper justification.


    • Sensitivity (columns Q to W)
      • Fill in columns Q to W based on the selected indicators.
      • Refer to 3.3.1 Menu of indicators for Natural Resource based Production Areas exposure units for the indicators on sensitivity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
      • The values (number or percentage) should preferably be based on the number or percentage of exposed land use to a particular hazard. For example, if the annual crop area exposed to high level flood is 50 hectares out of 500 hectares total forest area in the barangay, then the question to be answered should be, what is the percentage from the 50 hectare annual crop area exposed to high flood does not employ sustainable production technique?
      • OPTION: If there is no data at area specific level, the average of the barangay may be used. But this should be done with the assumption that the distribution of building or any other features is homogenous i.e. the percentage of farm not employing sustainable methodology in one part of the barangay is similar to all parts of the barangay.
      • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
      • The average sensitivity score shall be computed at the last column of sensitivity section (i.e. column W).
      • Refer to Sensitivity Scoring guide.


    • Adaptive capacity (columns X to AJ)
      • Fill in columns X to AJ based on the indicators of adaptive capacity.
      • Refer to 3.3.1 Menu of indicators for Natural Resource based Production Areas exposure units for the indicators on adaptive capacity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
      • Similar to indicators of sensitivities above, the values (number or percentage) should preferably be based on the number or percentage of exposed land use to a particular hazard.
      • OPTION: If there is no data at area specific level, the average of the barangay may be used. But this should be done with the assumption that the distribution of building or any other features is homogenous i.e. the percentage of farm with risk insurance in one part of the barangay is similar to all parts of the barangay.
      • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
      • The average adaptive capacity score shall be computed at the last column of adaptive capacity section (i.e. column AS)
      • Refer to Adaptive Capacity Scoring guide.


    • Once EDT for Natural Resource-based Production Areas are filled in, the succeeding columns for vulnerability (columns AN to AO) and risk (AP-AR) shall be automatically computed. The computation of vulnerability and risk scores are explained in Step 4 and Step 5.

Note

  • All columns must be filled in. Values for columns highlighted in yellow are automatically computed once values in the preceding sections are provided.
  • If number or percentage cannot be determined, ordinal level data (i.e. low, medium and high; poor, fair, good) can also be used.
  • Complete all Natural Resource-based Production Areas EDTs for all hazards (i.e. flood, landslide, storm surge, groundshaking, etc.).
  • Scores are explained in Step 5.


Natural Resource-based Production Areas EDM


The EDM shows the location and distribution of Natural Resource based Production Areas exposure units (i.e. fishpond area) and hazard prone areas (i.e. flood prone areas).


Instructions:


1. Identify and map Natural Resource based Production Areas.

    • Collect spatial data on the Natural Resource based Production Areas of the city/ municipality.
    • Natural Resource-based Production Areas may include, but are not limited to:
      • Crop area
      • Open and closed forest
      • Fishpond
      • Mangrove forest
    • Produce a map that shows the location of the Natural Resource based Production Areas. Aside from municipal-scaled map, LGUs are encouraged to produce barangay- or cluster-scaled map for better risk analysis.


2. Identify and map flood prone areas

    • Collect spatial data on the flood prone areas.
    • LGUs and other users are encouraged to use probabilistic hazard maps (refer to probabilistic hazard maps). Use spatial data on hazard maps collected in Step 1.2.


3. Assess exposure of Natural Resource-based Production Areas to flood hazard

    • Using GIS, overlay flood hazard map on the Natural Resource-based Production Areas data.
    • Utilize GIS geoprocessing tools such as "clipping" "select" and "statistics," among other geoprocessing tools, to identify Natural Resource-based Production Areas that are exposed to hazards.
      • This video  demonstrates the use of "intersect/clipping" and "statistics" to compute for the area of exposed Natural Resource-based Production Areas. The same method applies when assessing other exposure units that make use of polygon data.
    • Produce a map showing exposure of Natural Resource based Production Areas to flood.
