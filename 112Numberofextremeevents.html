1.1.2 Number of extreme events


The climate change projection by PAGASA in 2011 based on the Special Report on Emission Scenarios model (SRES) by Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) also suggests that communities and cities in the Philippines may likely to experience extreme events such as days with temperature exceeding 35Â°celsius, dry days with less than 2.5 mm rainfall, and days with extreme rainfall more than 150 mm. The model also suggests that under RCP 8.5, sea level may rise up to 0.82 meter by 2100 (PAGASA, 2011). The recent climate change projection based on CLIRAM by PAGASA, however, suggests that sea level will rise by approximately 20 cm by the end of the 21st century (PAGASA, 2018). LGUs and all stakeholders are encouraged to consult with PAGASA.
