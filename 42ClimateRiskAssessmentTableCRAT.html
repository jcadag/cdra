4.2 Climate Risk Assessment Table (CRA Table)

4.2 Climate Risk Assessment Table (CRA Table)


Instructions:


    • Identify climate variable (i.e. increasing temperature, excessive precipitation and sea-level rise) and general changes in climate variable (refer to the results of climate stimuli identification in Step 1)
    • Identify ecosystem (system of interest) and climate change impacts - this refers to particular ecosystem such as a coastal area, urban area, agriculture and forest (refer to the results of Impact Chain Analysis in Step 2)
    • Identify exposure units, sensitivity and adaptive capacity. The inputs to these columns may be copied and pasted from Step 3 EDTs. The entries may also be subjective i.e. qualitative assessment or short narrative about sensitivities or adaptive capacities.
    • Assess the vulnerabilities of each system of interest.
    • Determine sensitivity and sensitivity score using the suggested rating scale (columns F and G):


SCORES

CRITERIA

High (3)

Estimated direct impacts in terms of number of fatalities, injuries and value of property damage will be disastrous given the extent of exposure and current sensitivity of the system.

Moderate (2)

Moderate direct impacts in terms of terms of number of fatalities, injuries and value of property damage are expected given the extent of exposure and current sensitivities of the system.

Low (1)

Estimated direct and indirect impacts are low to negligible which can be felt within a short term period.



    • Determine adaptive capacity and adaptive capacity score using the suggested rating scale (columns H and I):
      •  - The system is able to accommodate changes in climate. There are adaptation measures in place to address impacts.
      •  - Addressing the impacts will require significant cost but it is still within the capacity of the system to adapt to potential impacts.
      • - The system is not  flexible to accommodate changes in the climate. Addressing the impacts will be costly. The LGU and property owners will require external assistance to address the impacts.


SCORES

CRITERIA

High (1)

Estimated direct impacts in terms of number of fatalities, injuries and value of property damage will be disastrous given the extent of exposure and current sensitivity of the system.

Moderate (2)

Moderate direct impacts in terms of terms of number of fatalities, injuries and value of property damage are expected given the extent of exposure and current sensitivities of the system.

Low (3)

Estimated direct and indirect impacts are low to negligible which can be felt within a short term period.



    • In columns J and K, compute for the vulnerability index by multiplying the Sensitivity and Adaptive Capacity Scores. Accordingly, based on the computed vulnerability index, categorize the index scores into categories:
      • High (3): > 6 – 9
      • Moderate (2): > 3 – 6
      • Low (1): < 3
