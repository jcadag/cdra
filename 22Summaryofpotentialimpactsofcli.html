2.2 Summary of potential impacts of climate stimuli and hazards on exposed units


This section summarizes the potential impacts of climate stimuli and hydro-meteorological and geological hazards.


Summary of potential impacts of hydro-meteorological and geological hazards


Instructions:


    • Fill in template tables below to understand impacts of climate stimuli and hazards on different systems of interest and exposure units.
    • List down the climate stimuli that could affect the city and the communities (column 1)
    • Provide details on general changes (column 2)
    • List down the systems of interest involved (column 3)
    • Identify the potential impacts (or results) of each climate stimuli (column 4)
    • Identify the 5 exposure units that may be affected by each climate stimuli (column 5). Exposure units refer to elements present within each ecosystem such as (refer to step 3 for more details about exposure units):
      • population
      • urban use areas
      • natural resource-based production areas
      • critical point facilities
      • lifeline facilities
    • Identify the type of risk assessment that should be conducted for each climate stimuli and concerned exposure units. The types of risk assessment are:
      • Climate Risk Assessment (CRA) (refer to Step 4)
      • Disaster Risk Assessment (DRA) (refer to Step 5)
