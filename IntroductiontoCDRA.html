Introduction to CDRA

What is CDRA?


CDRA stands for Climate and Disaster Risk Assessment. It is a methodology to understand and assess the potential impacts of hazards to people and their properties. CDRA also allows assessment of sensitivities and capacities of people and many other elements at risk (i.e. roads, buildings, natural resources, etc.) which largely determines vulnerability and eventually the outcomes of any disasters. CDRA goes beyond the conventional “risk assessment” as it incorporates climate-related hazards and their prospective impacts in the behavior or characteristics of hazards.


Why do LGUs need to engage in CDRA?


There are at least two reasons why LGUs need to engage in CDRA.  


Firstly, the law requires LGUs to comply with the provisions of RA 9729 (Climate Change Act of the Philippines of 2009) and RA 10121 (DRRM Act of the Philippines of 2010) which mandate mainstreaming of DRR and CCA in development planning. Concretely, CDRA should be a part of the CLUP and CDP formulation or updating based on HLURB's 3-volume guidebooks on CLUP.


Secondly, and in corollary to compliance to the laws, CDRA is an effective methodology to assess risk and reduce the impacts of disasters. The country is frequently affected by disasters associated with natural hazards. Exposure to hazards, increasing vulnerabilities of people and their properties and lack of capacities are among the contributory factors that increase risk of disasters. Recent experiences of disasters in the country (e.g. Typhoon Yolanda, Typhoon Pablo and Typhoon Ondoy) coupled with scientific studies point to climate changes as one of the main causes. CDRA should be able to capture that complexity of disaster risk. The result of CDRA can then be a solid basis for planning  by stakeholders of DRR and development works (e.g. local government authorities .


What are the benefits of mainstreaming?


1. Understand risk and risk factors i.e. exposure to hazards, sensitivities and adaptive capacities.

2. Identify priority decision areas and development challenges based on risk assessment.

3. Mainstream results of risk assessment to land use planning.
