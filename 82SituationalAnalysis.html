8.2 Situational Analysis

       Entry point to 12-Step CLUP Process:


Situational analysis is Step 4 of the 12-Step CLUP process. In relation to CDRA, this step should involve understanding the implications of climate change and hazards to the various development sectors such as:

    • Social sector
    • Economic sector
    • Infrastructure and utilities sector


In particular, situational analysis involves:


Adjustments to land demand projections


How many houses are exposed to hazards that need to be relocated? How many hectares of land are needed for residential expansion in less hazard prone areas considering the number of families to be relocated and projected increased in population? These are some of the questions that may be answered in situational analysis. Often, it involves adjustment to land supply in response to projections and results of risk and vulnerability assessment. Sectors that may be included for land adjustment may include but are not limited to (HLURB, 2015: p. 203):  


    • Housing
    • Education related facilities (i.e. primary, secondary and tertiary level schools)
    • Health related facilities (Hospitals, Municipal Health Stations/Centers)
    • Social welfare related facilities (day care centers, senior citizen centers, PWDs and women)
    • Government related facilities (i.e. Barangay Halls, Municipal Level Ofces
    • Commercial, Industrial, and other production related land uses;


Adjusted demand for lifeline utilities by type


Similarly, demand for lifeline utilities may be adjustment based on projected needs and present risk and vulnerability assessment. These lifeline utilities may include, but are not limited to (HLURB, 2015: p. 203):


    • Specific routes on where to allocate the estimated ideal road requirement such as evacuation routes, alternative/back-up routes
    • Key road segments/routes for climate proofing or rehabilitation
    • Demand for key water-related facilities/distribution networks (distribution line, water pumps, water districts, water storage, etc.)
    • Demand for power and communication distribution networks and support facilities
