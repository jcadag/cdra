1.3 Gather data on historical disaster damage and loss data


Experience of past disasters is a good source of information on the natural hazards that affect or likely to affect the city and its communities. While climate change projection provides a prospective view (i.e. future scenario) of climate-related hazards, historical disaster damage and loss data inventory provide us a retrospective view of the characteristics and behaviour of hazards during disaster events.


Instructions:


    • Identify the hazards and disasters that affected the city (or the barangays) in the past.
    • Refer to previously submitted Rapid Damage Assessment and Needs Analysis Forms (RDANA) and situational reports from the NDRRMC. Recent situational reports can be accessed from the NDRRMC website (http://www.ndrrmc.gov.ph/). Other possible sources are data from national and local government agencies such as M/CDRRMO, OCD, DSWD, or DILG.
    • In the absence of documentation of previous disasters or to complement existing ones, disaster history may be established using the template presented below (HLURB et al, 2015: p. 70).
