Step 9 - Monitoring and evaluating CDRA

       Entry point to 12-Step CLUP Process:


Monitoring and evaluating CDRA is crucial for at least two reasons: (1) ensure the quality of CDRA synthesis as a planning document, and (2) track the progress and achievement of mainstreaming DRR-CCA in CLUP and CDP by putting in place necessary indicators and mechanism for assessment purposes.


Step 9 provides procedure for assessment of CDRA using the following templates:

    • Review Tool for CDRA
    • Monitoring and Evaluation Indicators


The review tool for CDRA provides LGU a self-assessment tool to determine the quality of the CDRA synthesis based on the standard set by the supplemental guideline. The Monitoring and Evaluation Indicators focuses on the accomplishments of plans and actions  (i.e. policy interventions and actions for DRR-CCA) based on indicators as indicated in the CDRA Synthesis or CLUP and CDP document.


Review Tool for CDRA


    • For LGU's self-assessment, designate a review team from the CDRA TWG to assess the quality of CDRA methodology and synthesis using the Review Tool below.
    • Fill in the summary form of the review tool.
    • Provide a response to the questions on each row.
    • Provide a score based on subjective assessment.
    • Determine the average score and overall evaluation (i.e. Passed, Good, Very good, Excellent)

Comprehensiveness of CDRA (based on Chapter 3 of the HLURB (2014: p. 55-194) supplemental guideline)


  1. Does CDRA follow the 6-step procedure proposed by HLURB?





  1. Are all significant hazards considered in the hazard assessment?





  1. Are climate change projections included in the hazard scoping?





  1. Does building of exposure databases follow the procedure proposed in the HLURB guideline?  





  1. Does exposure database contain aggregated data on gender and other vulnerable sectors?





  1. Does exposure database contain the indicators (or similar and appropriate indicators) proposed in the HLURB guideline?





  1. Does CDRA make use of Geographic Information System or any mapping tools?





  1. Are the maps and tables properly analyzed?





Mainstreaming of CDRA to land use planning (based on Chapter 4 of the HLURB (2014: p. 197-269) supplemental guideline)


  1. Is the key information (and data) derived from 6-step CDRA (i.e. p. 202 of supplemental guideline) proactively incorporated in the implementation of 12-step process of CLUP?  





  1. Is DRR including CCA properly considered in crafting the vision for CLUP (p. 228 of supplemental guideline)?





  1. Are results of CDRA incorporated in the sectoral planning (i.e. social, economic, infrastructure and utilities sector, etc.) (p. 204 of supplemental guideline)?





  1. Are results of CDRA integrated in Land Demand and Supply Analysis (p. 218 of supplemental guideline)?  





  1. Are results of CDRA used to determine or influence the plans for the four general land use policy areas (urban, production, protection, and infrastructure and utilities) (p. 241 of the supplemental guideline)?





  1. Are Programs, Projects, and Activities (including legislations) risk informed?





  1. Does zoning provisions articulated in the zoning ordinance consider the hazards and risks in the city and communities?





  1. Does zoning provisions articulated in the zoning ordinance consider all risk sensitive spatial strategies (p. 236 to 249 of the supplemental guideline)?





Format of the presentation of results of CDRA (based on Part 3 of Volume 1 – Planning Process (HLURB, 2013: p. 236-234))


  1. Does CDRA has separate synthesis (or writeup) which summarizes the key findings of 6-step CDRA?





  1. Are maps and tables derived from CDRA of good quality (considering resolution and layout)?





  1. Are the results of CDRA properly integrated in the sections and subsections of the proposed outline for CLUP (Part 3 of Volume 1 – Planning Process; p. 236)?  





Bottom-up, gender and socially-inclusive planning process (based on USAID-SURGE case studies on CDRA)


  1. Does CDRA and CLUP at the city level consider the plans and data at the barangay level?





  1. Does CDRA consider the specific exposure to hazards, vulnerabilities and capacities of gender minorities, children, and other marginalized groups in the communities?





  1. Does the conduct of CDRA (or CLUP) allow the participation of local authorities and people including the marginalized sectors (i.e. women, children, PWD, etc.) at the barangay level?
