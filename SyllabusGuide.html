Syllabus Guide

Syllabus guide for CDRA


All training should ensure that steps (activities), learning objectives, content and learning methods of CDRA are considered. The table below presents a syllabus guide for CDRA. The steps also serve as the flowchart for the activities required to accomplish CDRA (check sample program provided below).


Table i: Syllabus guide for CDRA

Introduction to CDRA
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Acknowledge self-awareness on where the Philippine Legal and Policy Framework to address Climate Change and Hazards/ Disasters.
  • Define and explain the concepts of climate change and natural hazards.
  • Discuss differences of terminologies (risk, vulnerability, sensitivity, exposure and adaptive capacity).
  • Legal Framework (RA 10121, RA 9279, etc.)
  • International Frameworks on CCA-DRR (Paris Agreement, Sustainable Development Goals, Sendai Framework for DRR)
  • Basic concepts and definitions of climate change and hazards
  • Overview and historical trends on climate change and disasters
  • Different types of hazards
  • Potential impacts and implications of climate change and hazards
  • CCA-DRR in a Ridge-to-Reef/IEM perspective
  • Risk and vulnerability framework and terminologies (IPCC, UNFCCC, UNISDR, Assessment Report 5)
  • Definitions on:
  • Likelihood of occurrence, exposure, susceptibility, sensitivity vulnerability, adaptive capacity, etc.
  • Climate change adaptation and mitigation, disaster risk reduction and management, disaster risk mitigation
  • Group Activity (what hazards affect your locality?)
  • Group dynamics: Getting started- LGUs brainstorm on own locality regarding the climate change, hazards and their impacts in a ridge-to-reef/IEM perspective
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Game on adaptation – egg game
  • Quiz on terminologies and definitions
  • Videos

Introduction to CDRA
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Describe and apply  how the CCA/DRR has been mainstreamed in the CLUP Process  
  • Identify the differentiation/relationship of CCA/DRR (convergence)
  • Gain awareness and basic understanding on the Climate and Disaster Risk Assessment (CDRA) process
  • Goals of incorporating CCA/DRR in the CLUP
  • Concept of mainstreaming
  • Mainstreaming framework for CCA-DRR into the CLUP (CDRA outputs as input to the 12-step CLUP process)
  • CCA-DRR Convergence and Linkages Framework
  • Overview on the CDRA Process
  • Inputs and expected outputs of CDRA
  • Importance of CDRA in CLUP and other local planning documents
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Video on Climate Change in Land Use Planning “We know enough”
  • Group dynamics - Review of existing plans and other CCA-DRR related documents
  • Open forum and discussions

Module A - Assembling CDRA


Step 1 - Collect and organize climate change and hazard information
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Gain understanding on the existing and projected climate and hazard conditions of their locality
  • Interpret climate and hazard information provided by National Government Agencies (Collective Strengthening of Community Awareness to Natural Disasters – CSCAND Agencies)
  • Organize climate and hazard information that will serve as inputs to the CDRA



  • PAGASA Climate Change Projections (SRES and RCP) and interpretation
  • Hazard Maps and their interpretation
  • Deterministic and probabilistic hazard maps
  • Magnitude, speed of onset, susceptibility, frequency, duration and areas covered by hazards
  • Scale, format and sources of hazard maps (community-based, participatory and official maps)
  • Table and data requirements (hazard inventory, climate change projections, records of disasters, and hazard susceptibility)
  • Validation of climate and hazard data
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Group exercises on table preparation
  • Workshop/open discussions on climate projections and hazard information interpretation
  • Distribution of official and available climate and hazard data

Step 2 - Scope the potential impacts of hazards and climate change
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Identify climate change impacts that affect and might affect their locality.
  • Determine linkages and connections between the various climate change impacts and hazards.
  • Determine data and information for risk and vulnerability assessment.
  • Identify possible policy options for climate actions.
  • Climate change impact chain analysis (per ecosystems, systems of interests, or sector)
  • Types of impacts:
  • Primary, secondary, tertiary impacts
  • Biophysical, socio-economic impacts
  • Positive and negative impacts
  • Climate Impact Chain Summary Matrix
  • Sample climate impact chains
  • Extended climate impact chain assessment for ecosystems and other slow-creeping impacts (impact chain for determining sensitivity, adaptive capacity and exposure indicators)
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Workshop on climate impact chain analysis using metacards
  • Video on the impacts of climate change “El Gamma Penumbra dance video”

Step 3 - Develop exposure database
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Develop a GIS-based exposure database that will serve as inputs to the risk and vulnerability assessments.
  • Determine additional data requirements and needs based on the review of the database and information gathered from the climate impact chain analysis.
  • Exposure elements (Population, Urban Use, Natural Resource-based Use, Lifeline Utilities, Critical Facilities)
  • Sensitivity indicators per exposure element (e.g. Population – percentage of ISF, percentage of families living below poverty threshold, etc.)
  • Adaptive Capacity indicators per exposure element (e.g. Population – access to information, access to financial assistance, etc.)
  • Exposure database matrices/templates
  • Exposure database map requirements
  • Other tools, systems and methods in aid to exposure database development (CBMS, REDAS, Climex.DB, Qlik Sense)
  • Climate impact chain outputs
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Group presentations of exposure database
  • One-on-one review of exposure database
  • GIS demos and trainings

Module B - Building CDRA


Step 4 - Climate Change Vulnerability Assessment (CCVA)


Step 5 - Disaster Risk Assessment (DRA)
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Understand the concepts and processes of risk estimation and vulnerability scoring.
  • Determine the level of risks and vulnerabilities of the various exposure elements based on the collected data.
  • Develop risk and vulnerability maps.
  • Outputs and results of the previous 3 CDRA steps
  • Climate and hazard information
  • Climate impact chains
  • Exposure database
  • Risk estimation and scoring
  • Likelihood of occurrence X severity of consequence
  • Vulnerability scoring
  • Degree of impact X adaptive capacity
  • Risk and vulnerability scoring parameters (e.g. likelihood of occurrence – based on return period)
  • Other risk estimation techniques (e.g. Risk Analysis Project risk estimation based on land uses and building structure type)
  • GIS-based risk analysis and mapping
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Group exercise on table preparation, scoring and analysis
  • Group presentation of results
  • GIS demos and trainings

Step 6 - Summarize findings
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Identify priority decision areas that shall impact the development of the city/municipality and that shall require climate change adaptation and disaster risk reduction measures.
  • Summarize results into a summary matrix table.
  • Determine appropriate intervention measures and policy options based on CDRA findings.
  • Write and present CDRA outputs and results.
  • Outputs and results of the previous 3 CDRA steps
  • Climate and hazard information
  • Climate impact chains
  • Exposure database
  • Risk estimation
  • Risk reduction options
  • Risk avoidance
  • Risk transfer/sharing
  • Risk mitigation
  • Risk acceptance
  • Climate change adaptation and mitigation
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Group exercises and discussion
  • Writeshops
  • GIS demos and trainings

Module C - Compiling CDRA


Step 7 - Producing CDRA Compilation
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Produce outline for the CDRA Synthesis
  • Summarize the findings of the entire CDRA Process (Step 1-6) into a synthesis document
  • CDRA Synthesis
  • Writeshop

Step 8 - Mainstreaming CDRA in CLUP
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Mainstream climate change adaptation and disaster risk reduction into the CLUP and ZO.
  • CDRA Results
  • CCA-DRR Entry points to the 12-Step CLUP Process
  • Sieve Mapping and Suitability Analysis
  • Sample Format of CDRA Report/CDRA Inputs to the Sectoral Studies/Ecological Profile
  • Adjusted Land Supply and Demand
  • PowerPoint lectures
  • Group exercises and discussion
  • Writeshops
  • GIS demos and trainings

Step 9 - Monitoring and evaluating CDRA
  • Participants will be able to:
  • Assess the quality of the CDRA Synthesis using a review tool
  • Monitor the accomplishments of plans and actions (i.e. policy interventions and actions for DRR-CCA) based on indicators as indicated in the Monitoring and Evaluation Tool
  • Review tool for CDRA
  • Monitoring and Evaluation Tool
  • Review form and checklist
  • Indicators for monitoring and evaluation
