4.1 Theoretical formulation of CRA


The AR4 Framework (basis of Supplemental Guidelines by HLURB on CDRA) assesses vulnerability based on 2 main factors: (1) exposure to hazard and (2) sensitivities and adaptive capacities. Exposure to hazard is determined by (1) magnitude of hazards and (2) exposure of elements at risk. In the AR5 Framework, the operational definition of vulnerability was revised and integrated in disaster risk assessment process. As a result, instead of vulnerability assessment (i.e. Climate Change Vulnerability Assessment or CCVA), the assessment shifted to climate risk where vulnerability is one of the two components. The definition has also changed where vulnerability is defined as sensitivities and capacities (coping and adaptive), and exposure as a separate component. Note: The approved supplemental guideline considers exposure under “vulnerability”. But based on AR5, vulnerability consists only of sensitivities and adaptive capacities. LGUs are encouraged to follow the AR5 Framework.


When to apply CRA


In theory, CRA may be conducted when the hazards and exposure units involve are:

  • Climate change stimuli such as temperature and precipitation change and sea level rise, and other slow-onset and creeping hazards (e.g. drought)
  • Exposure units involve are not included in the five exposure units (e.g. corals)
