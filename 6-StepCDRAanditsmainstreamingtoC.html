6-Step CDRA and its mainstreaming to CLUP


Looking at the bigger picture, CDRA does not start and end with the 6-step CDRA as described in the previous section. The results of 6-Step CDRA must then be organized and compiled and eventually mainstreamed in CLUP. CDRA must guide and must be guided by the kinds of development being envisioned by all concerned stakeholders during the planning process particularly in land use planning. Thus, in practice, CDRA should not be treated as a separate process to the CLUP process proposed in the first three volumes of HLURBâ€™s Guidelines to Comprehensive Land Use Planning. Concretely, CDRA process (column 1) must be a part of the wider 12-Step CLUP Process (column 2 of table below). CDRA and its mainstreaming to CDP, LDRRMP and LCCAP


CDRA is also useful to other planning documents such as CDP, LDRRMP and LCCAP. These planning activities require inclusion of risk assessment as basis of plans. In fact, risk assessment should be consistent in all these plans meaning risk assessment should be based on a single methodology i.e. CDRA. In relation to this, the Department of Interior and Local Government Unit (DILG) has issued Memorandum Circular 2015-77 to advise LGUs to mainstream DRR-CCA in development planning.
