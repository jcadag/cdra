8.5 Selection of the Preferred Spatial Strategy

       Entry point to 12-Step CLUP Process:


Spatial strategies refer to the "detailed allocation and location of the various land use categories" reflecting risk reduction and management principles. These principles are summarized in the table below:

 

A. Risk Avoidance or Elimination

    • Change of location of existing exposed settlements or locating new expansion areas outside of potential hazard susceptible areas. Possible increase in frequency and severity of hazards due to climate change (i.e. storm surge, oods, sea-level rise) should be considered in conceptualizing and implementing these strategies. Some of these strategies include, but are not limited to:  
      • encouraging open spaces
      • establishment and extension of buffer easements (i.e. coastal, river)
      • compliance to/ strict enforcement of nationally prescribed easement regulations

B. Risk Reduction through Mitigation

    • Measures for mitigation (or adaptation) that can be applied to reduce potential risks/vulnerabilities by changing physical characteristics or operations of a system or the element exposed to hazards. This is the alternative strategy when risk avoidance or elimination is not preferred. Some of these strategies include, but are not limited to:  
      • Mitigation - policy regulations e.g. imposing building design regulations; engineering-based measures e.g. flood control, sea-wall, slope stabilization; nonstructural mitigation e.g. rehabilitation of upland and coastal forests to reduce hazards.
      • Duplication or Redundancy - backup support for systems or facilities that may become nonfunctional/operational after a hazard impact.  e.g. establishment of alternate transportation routes, looping and back-up systems for water distribution, and establishing alternative critical point facilities such as schools and hospitals.
      • Spatial separation - strategic placement of critical facilities to reduce exposure to hazards e.g. avoidance of locating health, educational, commercial, governance-based facilities/services in one place that is exposed to hazard
      • Preparedness measures  - non-structural measures e.g. improving capability to rescue, salvage, and recover; installation of early warning systems; increasing level of awareness through information, education, and communication (IEC) programs; and developing contingency/evacuation plans.

C. Risk sharing or risk transfer

    • Use of financial and economic measures to transfer or share risk with other entities such as insurance companies. In case of hazard impact, insurance companies can cover and pay for damages based on the agreed coverage indicated in the insurance policy.

D. Risk retention or acceptance  

    • “Do-nothing” scenario where risks are fully accepted. In this strategy, people and institutions that may be affected by hazards should have the capacity to recover using its own resources.


Instructions:


    • Conduct selection of preferred spatial strategies (Table 8.5.1)
    • Identify at least three spatial strategies (Alternative 1-3)
    • List down criteria for selection based on the different criteria that addresses climate and disaster risks
    • Provide weight for each criteria
    • Provide a rating (1-4) for each alternative using the following scoring guide:
      • 1 - Low contribution to the achievement of desired goals, very high cost requirements to achieve goals, very limited capacities and/or capabilities of constituents or the government to achieve goals
      • 2 - Moderate contribution to the achievement of desired goals, high cost requirements to achieve goals, limited capacities and/or capabilities of constituents or the government to achieve goals
      • 3 - High contribution to the achievement of desired goals, moderate cost requirements to achieve goals, existing capacities and/or capabilities of constituents or the government to achieve goals are present
      • 4 - Very high contribution to the achievement of desired goals, Low cost requirements to achieve goals, existing capacities and/or capabilities of constituents or the government to achieve goals are highly compatible
    • Compute for the weighted rating
    • Compute for the total rating
    • Rank the preferred development thrusts according to the result
