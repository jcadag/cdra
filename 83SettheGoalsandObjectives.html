8.3 Set the Goals and Objectives

       Entry point to 12-Step CLUP Process:


Setting the goals and objectives is Step 5 of the 12-Step CLUP process. It supports the realization of the long term visions (refer to Section 8.1 Set the Vision). These terms are defined these terms as (HLURB, 2015: p. 228):


    • Goals - typically intersectoral in scope and long to medium term in time scale that seek to address or respond to a general problem.
    • Objectives - more specific statements of medium to short-term targets which seek to address specic sectoral problems that would contribute in the achievement of the identified goal.


Instructions


    • Copy and paste visions from Section 8.1 Set the vision  (column A)
    • Identify specific goals. Stakeholders may restate development challenges/issues, including vision descriptors, as goal statements  (column B)
    • Identify specific objectives. Stakeholders may restate policy interventions identified in the sectoral studies as objectives (column C).
    • The mainstreaming of CDRA in this step means that goals and objectives of CLUP should be risk sensitive. This means that:  
      • Ensure optimum economic productivity through resilient and well-adapted production systems
      • Address vulnerabilities/sensitivities and enhance adaptive capacities of the population
      • Ensuring the uninterrupted access to social support services
      • Efficient linkage/access, and distribution systems through the establishment of climate proofed and resilient infrastructure and utilities
      • More efficient use of public and private investments
