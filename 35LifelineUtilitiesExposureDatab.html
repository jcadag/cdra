3.5 Lifeline Utilities Exposure Database

3.5 Lifeline Utilities Exposure Database


Establish Lifeline Utilities exposure database :


  • Lifeline utilities EDT (Exposure Database Table) - produce and complete table that consist proper indicators to assess exposure to hazards and risk for lifeline utilities.
  • Lifeline Utilities EDM (Exposure Database Maps) - produce maps that show location and distribution of lifeline utilities and hazards (preferably probabilistic hazard maps).


Lifeline Utilities EDT


The EDT compiles the indicators for vulnerability and risk computation for all exposure units in all barangays exposed to particular hazards. It is a long table that requires inputs such as statistics, GIS-derived and formula-based values. Watch this video  for an orientation on the EDT.


Instructions:


1. EDT has four major criteria that are used to compute vulnerability and risk:

    • Hazard
    • Exposure
    • Sensitivity
    • Adaptive Capacity


2. Identify indicators for each criterion (refer to sample lifeline utilities exposure units database EDT above)

    • HLURB has already proposed indicators for each criterion. LGUs are encouraged to change or replace these indicators depending on their context.
    • Refer to 3.5.1 Menu of indicators for lifeline facilities exposure units. Other indicators may be included or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.


3. Fill in the lifeline utilities EDT

    • Barangay and lifeline utilities (column A-B)
      • List only those barangays and lifeline utilities that are exposed to the particular hazard being assessed (e.g. flood in this example; and all lifeline utilities exposed to flood e.g. roads, powerlines, etc.).
    • Hazard (columns C to F)
      • Fill in columns C to F. Watch this video  for a demonstration.
      • Refer to Hazard Scoring guide.
    • Exposure (columns G to N)
      • Fill in columns G to N based on actual data on land use in the barangay (e.g. total barangay allocation for critical point facilities).
      • Exposure score is auto computed based on the formula. Refer to Exposure Score guide. The formula can be adjusted with proper justification.


    • Sensitivity (columns O to W)
      • Fill in columns O to W based on the selected indicators.
      • Refer to 3.5.1 Menu of indicators for lifeline utilities exposure units for the indicators on sensitivity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
      • The values (number or percentage) should preferably be based on the number or percentage of exposed lifeline utilities to a particular hazard.
      • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
      • The average sensitivity score shall be computed at the last column of sensitivity section (i.e. column W).
      • Refer to Sensitivity Scoring guide.


    • Adaptive capacity (columns X to AN)
      • Fill in columns X to AN based on the indicators of adaptive capacity.
      • Refer to 3.5.2 Menu of indicators for lifeline utilities exposure units for the indicators on adaptive capacity. Other indicators may be added or proxy data may be proposed if data for suggested indicators are not available.
      • Similar to indicators of sensitivities above, the values (number or percentage) should preferably be based on the number or percentage of exposed critical facilities to a particular hazard.
      • The score for each indicator shall be automatically computed.
      • The average adaptive capacity score shall be computed at the last column of adaptive capacity section (i.e. column AN)
      • Refer to Adaptive Capacity Scoring guide.


    • Once EDT for urban use are filled in, the succeeding columns for vulnerability (columns AJ to AK) and risk (AL-AN) shall be automatically computed. The computation of vulnerability and risk scores are explained in Step 4 and Step 5.

Lifeline Utilities EDM


The EDM shows the location and distribution of lifeline utilities exposure units (i.e. roads, powerlines, etc.) and hazard prone areas (i.e. flood prone areas).


Instructions:


1. Identify and map lifeline utilities  

    • Collect spatial data on the lifeline utilities of the city/ municipality.
    • Critical point facilities may include, but are not limited to:
      • roads, and water; power and communication lines and networks
    • Produce a map that shows the location of the lifeline utilities. Aside from municipal-scaled map, LGUs are encouraged to produce barangay- or cluster-scaled map for better risk analysis.


2. Identify and map flood prone areas

    • Collect spatial data on the flood prone areas.
    • LGUs and other users are encouraged to use probabilistic hazard maps (refer to probabilistic hazard maps). Use spatial data on hazard maps collected in Step 1.2.


3. Assess exposure of critical point facilities to flood hazard

    • Using GIS, overlay flood hazard map on the lifeline utilities.
    • Utilize GIS geoprocessing tools such as "intersect/clipping" "select" and "statistics," among other geoprocessing tools, to identify lifeline utilities that are exposed to hazards.
      • This video  demonstrates the use of "intersect/clipping" and "statistics" to compute for the length or area of exposed lifeline utilities. The same method applies when assessing other exposure units that make use of polygon data.
    • Produce a map showing exposure of lifeline utilities to flood.
